North Reading, MA

Long ago, in 1651, people started to live in an area near a river called Ipswich. They got permission to settle there from a town called Reading. In 1853, this area officially became its own town called North Reading. North Reading is next to other towns like Andover, North Andover, Middleton, Lynnfield, Reading, and Wilmington. Back then, about 350 years ago, a few families came to live here. They had big farms near the river. They mostly grew food to eat, like vegetables. There was also a sawmill to cut wood by a pond in 1694, and later, more mills to grind grains and cut wood. Around 1820, people started to make shoes, and by 1850, almost every house had a small place to make shoes. They made shoes that were not expensive and sold them to people far away. During the Civil War in the 1860s, things changed. The town's shoe business went down, but they still made things like milk, apples, and other fruits in the 1900s. That's how North Reading grew from small farms to a town making different things over time!
